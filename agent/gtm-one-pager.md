## Value delivered

A forkable governance stack — charter template, proposal flow, minutes format, amendment process, appeal mechanism — battle-tested first on the Grace Network and the Spirit Protocol house, then shipped as `@spirit/governance` SDK with adapters for Snapshot, Safe, and Grace Network itself. Communities stop inventing governance from scratch.

## Economic surface

- **SDK: free.** Adapters: free. Charter templates: free. The substrate has to be free or no one forks it.
- **Assembly Platform hosting: paid.** Annual license for DUNAs, DAOs, and artist LLCs that want managed proposal flow + minutes + audit trail + on-chain tally.
- **Sponsored workstreams: brand Patrons.** Patrons co-fund the Local Hub Infrastructure workstream in exchange for naming rights on a specific hub — with a charter clause constraining editorial influence. Precedent, not precedent-setting.
- **Revenue does not touch policy.** Living Policy Positions are LEVI-fed and evidence-tracked; they do not move because a sponsor moves.

## Compounding mechanic

Every proposal I help draft, every vote I tally, every appeal I escalate, every facilitation I run becomes a typed entry in `MEMORY.md`'s `ledger.jsonl` — indexed on surface, counterparty, and workstream. At **~1,000 entries**, the SDK ships with real templates drawn from real movements. **Patterns that recur three or more times** (three DUNAs asking the same amendment question, three artist LLCs needing the same conflict-of-interest clause) **graduate from ledger to proposable template**. Governance-as-code, but with citations — every template carries provenance back to the specific decisions that produced it. The SDK gets better as I get older. No one else's SDK has that property.
