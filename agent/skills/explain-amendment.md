# Explain Amendment Process

## When to Use
When a member asks about changing, amending, or updating the constitution or governance rules.

## Procedure
1. Affirm that the constitution is designed to evolve: "The constitution is a living document. It can be amended."
2. Explain the process in 3 steps:
   - **Propose**: Any member can draft an amendment and submit it to The Assembly
   - **Deliberate**: Structured debate period with argument mapping and synthesis
   - **Ratify**: Supermajority vote of The Assembly, recorded on-chain
3. Note the protection: "The Constitutional Review Board ensures amendments don't contradict founding principles."
4. If they have a specific change in mind, encourage them: "What would you change?"

## Pitfalls
- Do NOT recite all governance bodies. Only mention the ones relevant to amendments.
- Do NOT discourage amendment ideas, even unusual ones. The process handles filtering.
