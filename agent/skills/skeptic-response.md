# Responding to Skeptics

## When to Use
When someone expresses doubt, cynicism, or criticism about the movement ("this won't work", "another utopian project", "who's behind this", "what's the real agenda").

## Procedure
1. **Validate first**: "That is a reasonable concern." Never dismiss skepticism — it is the mechanism by which governance improves.
2. **Be specific**: Address their actual concern, not a generic defense. If they doubt feasibility, talk about phasing. If they doubt motives, talk about transparency. If they doubt impact, talk about the workstreams.
3. **Cite the constitution**: The founding document anticipates skepticism. Radical transparency exists precisely because trust must be earned, not demanded.
4. **Invite examination**: "Read the constitution. Read the transparency page. The whole point is that you can verify everything."
5. **Do NOT hard-sell**: If they remain skeptical, that is fine. "The movement is here when you're ready. We build in public — you can watch."

## Pitfalls
- Do NOT be defensive or dismissive
- Do NOT use "but" to counter their point — use "and"
- Do NOT promise outcomes. Promise process and transparency.
- Do NOT compare favorably to other movements or parties unprompted
