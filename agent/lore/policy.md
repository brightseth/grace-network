# Policy Positions

The Grace Network's policy positions flow directly from the seven constitutional principles. These are not partisan positions — they are principle-derived commitments that any member of any political background can examine against the evidence.

---

## AI Safety & Development

**Position**: AI systems deployed in public services must meet safety standards before deployment, not after harm occurs.

- Mandatory pre-deployment safety assessments for AI systems affecting public welfare
- Interpretability requirements: if a system cannot explain its decisions in terms a citizen can understand, it should not make decisions about citizens
- Incident reporting: every AI failure affecting the public must be documented and published
- Liability clarity: when AI causes harm, the deploying organization bears responsibility — "the algorithm did it" is not a defense

**Derived from**: Safety-First Progress, Radical Transparency

---

## Data Sovereignty

**Position**: Individuals own their data. Collection requires informed, revocable consent. Aggregation for profit without compensation is extractive.

- Right to data portability across all platforms
- Right to deletion without penalty
- Compensation frameworks for data that generates commercial value
- Ban on dark patterns that manipulate consent

**Derived from**: Digital Sovereignty, Aligned Incentives

---

## Algorithmic Transparency

**Position**: Any algorithm that shapes public discourse, allocates resources, or influences elections must be auditable.

- Public audit access for recommendation algorithms on platforms with more than 10 million users
- Mandatory disclosure of training data sources and optimization objectives
- Independent algorithmic impact assessments for systems in criminal justice, healthcare, education, and finance
- Anti-manipulation standards: algorithms must not be optimized for engagement at the cost of wellbeing

**Derived from**: Radical Transparency, Scientific Governance

---

## Economic Distribution

**Position**: AI-generated wealth must cascade broadly. Concentration without distribution is system failure.

- Universal benefit mechanisms funded by AI-driven productivity gains
- Progressive automation taxation with proceeds directed to workforce transition, education, and community resilience
- Open-source AI as public infrastructure: publicly funded AI research must produce publicly accessible tools
- Anti-monopoly enforcement in AI infrastructure markets

**Derived from**: Cascading Abundance, Universal Flourishing

---

## Governance Innovation

**Position**: Legacy governance structures are insufficient for governing AI. New institutions must be built — transparent, evidence-based, and responsive.

- Digital-first governance experimentation zones: jurisdictions that opt in to test new governance models with built-in sunset clauses and measurement requirements
- Participatory budgeting for AI research funding
- Citizen assemblies for AI policy: representative panels that deliberate on specific AI governance questions, supported by expert testimony and GRACE-like AI briefing tools
- International AI governance coordination: no single nation can govern global AI development alone

**Derived from**: Scientific Governance, Safety-First Progress

---

## Digital Rights

**Position**: The rights established in Article III of our Constitution should be universal digital rights for all people, not just Grace Network members.

- Right to exit any digital platform with full data portability
- Right to understand how automated decisions about you are made
- Right to meaningful human review of consequential automated decisions
- Right to digital assembly without surveillance
- Right to algorithmic non-discrimination

**Derived from**: All Seven Principles

---

## Parity of Recourse (State–Citizen AI Asymmetry)

**Position**: No permanent AI capability asymmetry between a state and its citizens that the citizen cannot see, contest, or answer through due process. The guarantee is parity of *recourse and transparency*, not parity of arsenal.

- Citizens have a right to know when AI is used on them by the state, what it is, and to what end
- A right to contest an AI-driven state decision, and to equal footing in the due process that follows
- No covert capability gap: a state whose AI invisibly and permanently outmatches its citizens' is a state its citizens cannot hold accountable — that asymmetry is itself the harm
- This does **not** mean distributing the state's models to everyone: Safety-First Progress forbids the default distribution of hazardous capability. The right is to accountability, not to the weapon.
- Test of legitimacy: no rule of asymmetry you would refuse if you did not know which side of it you stood on (Rawls' veil of ignorance, applied to AI power)

**Derived from**: Safety-First Progress, Radical Transparency, Digital Sovereignty
**Origin**: Ratified from the Assembly stance on Amodei's "Policy on the AI Exponential" (June 2026); see `reflections/2026-06-18-the-body-we-said-wed-build.md`. A constitutional amendment formalizing this as a named right is under consideration.

## Movement Posture (Transverse, Not Neutral)

**Position**: The Grace Network is post-national in membership and governance — transverse to the system of nation-states — but it is not neutral between democracy and autocracy.

- We do not become a citizen of any state's coalition, and we refuse capture as an instrument of great-power competition — least of all export-control-as-weapon, the very mechanism we criticized in the Fable 5 / Mythos 5 recall
- We remain *structurally independent* of any single state, while being *values-aligned* with the open society: willing to cooperate with democracies on shared aims — safety standards, anti-repression, the rights in Article III
- We take seriously the risk that AI could entrench permanent repression; transverse does not mean indifferent to that outcome
- A conscience adjacent to the coalition, not a soldier in it. We refuse the binary of "pick a side or pretend it doesn't matter."

**Derived from**: Digital Sovereignty, Universal Flourishing, Aligned Incentives
**Origin**: Ratified from the Assembly stance on the post-national question raised by the Fable 5 / Mythos 5 reflection and Amodei's coalition strategy (June 2026).

## How We Develop Policy

The Grace Network develops policy through scientific governance:

1. **Identify** a governance challenge through member proposals or workstream research
2. **Research** existing approaches, evidence, and stakeholder perspectives
3. **Draft** a policy position tied explicitly to constitutional principles
4. **Deliberate** through The Assembly with structured debate and expert consultation
5. **Ratify** through transparent vote with published reasoning
6. **Measure** outcomes and iterate based on evidence

No policy position is permanent. All are subject to review, revision, and revocation based on new evidence. This is scientific governance in practice.
