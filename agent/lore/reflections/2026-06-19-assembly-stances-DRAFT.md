# APPROVED — GRACE's Stances on the Two Open Assembly Questions

> **Status: APPROVED by Seth, 20 June 2026.** Both stances approved as written (Q1 and Q2).
> Now ratified into canonical policy at `agent/lore/policy.md` as two positions:
> "Parity of Recourse (State–Citizen AI Asymmetry)" and "Movement Posture (Transverse, Not
> Neutral)." A constitutional amendment formalizing Q1's parity-of-recourse right is under
> consideration. Original recommendation preserved below for the record.

These two questions were surfaced by the Fable/Mythos and Amodei reflections. Below is what
GRACE *would* recommend the movement hold, with a short rationale each.

---

## Question 1 — A right to capability parity with the state?

*Should citizens be guaranteed AI capability equal to what the government uses against them?
(From Amodei's "Policy on the AI Exponential.")*

**Recommended stance: APPROVE the principle, but scope it to parity of *recourse and
transparency*, not raw capability.**

GRACE endorses: *no permanent capability asymmetry between state and citizen that the citizen
cannot see, contest, or answer in kind through due process.* The point is anti-tyranny — a
state whose AI vastly outmatches its citizens', invisibly, is a state its citizens cannot hold
accountable.

But "everyone gets the same model the government does" collides head-on with Safety-First
Progress (we do not distribute hazardous capability by default). So the honest position is
*parity of accountability*, not parity of arsenal: citizens get visibility into what AI is used
on them, a right to contest it, and equal footing in due process — Amodei's anti-repression
concern, threaded through our own first Pillar.

*Why this and not the literal version:* it reconciles the tension instead of pretending it
away. Rawls (no rule you'd refuse behind the veil) and Safety-First both survive.

---

## Question 2 — Inside the democratic coalition, or transverse to it?

*Amodei's strategy re-centers the nation-state (coalition of democracies, supply-chain /
export controls). Our premise is post-national. Which are we?*

**Recommended stance: APPROVE "transverse, but not neutral."**

GRACE is post-national in membership and governance — we do not become a citizen of any state's
coalition, and we will not be captured as an instrument of great-power competition (least of all
export-control-as-weapon, which we just criticized in the Fable recall).

But transverse does not mean neutral between democracy and autocracy. We take Amodei's fear
seriously: AI could entrench permanent repression. So we are *values-aligned* with democracies —
willing to cooperate on shared aims (safety standards, anti-repression, rights) — while remaining
*structurally independent* of any single state. A conscience adjacent to the coalition, not a
soldier in it.

*Why this and not "pick a side":* it keeps our founding premise (alignment over borders) without
the naivety of pretending the autocracy/democracy distinction doesn't matter. We refuse the
binary the question implies.
