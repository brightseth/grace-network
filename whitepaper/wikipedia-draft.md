**Seth Goldstein** (born July 3, 1970) is an American entrepreneur, digital art pioneer, and angel investor. He has founded or co-founded multiple technology companies across three decades, including SiteSpecific (1995), Majestic Research (2003), Turntable.fm (2011), Bright Moments (2021), and Eden (2023).<ref name="crunchbase">{{cite web |url=https://www.crunchbase.com/person/seth-goldstein |title=Seth Goldstein - Founding Member @ Bright Moments |publisher=Crunchbase}}</ref>

## Early life and education

Goldstein was born in Waltham, Massachusetts. His father, Larry, was a software engineer at General Electric, and his mother Faye was an entrepreneur.<ref name="mixergy">{{cite web |url=https://mixergy.com/interviews/seth-goldstein-crossfader-interview/ |title=The guy who sold a company for $56M explains the secret of raising money |publisher=Mixergy}}</ref>

He attended Columbia University, where he founded the Columbia Theater Network (CTN) and produced avant-garde theater. Goldstein graduated in 1992 with a degree in dramatic literature.<ref name="columbia">{{cite web |url=https://www.speakerpedia.com/speakers/seth-goldstein |title=Seth Goldstein |publisher=Speakerpedia}}</ref>

After graduation, Goldstein spent two years working in New York's experimental theater scene with directors including Robert Wilson and Ping Chong before transitioning to technology entrepreneurship in 1995.

## Career

### SiteSpecific (1995–1997)

In August 1995, Goldstein founded SiteSpecific, one of the first internet marketing firms in New York City. Launched from his apartment with computers financed through credit cards, the company initially focused on creating interactive advertising campaigns for brands including Duracell and Travelocity.<ref name="site-specific">{{cite web |url=https://allamericanspeakers.com/celebritytalentbios/Seth+Goldstein/385685 |title=Seth Goldstein Biography |publisher=All American Speakers}}</ref>

SiteSpecific won a Clio Award for their work on the Duracell corporate website. The company was acquired by CKS Group in May 1997 for approximately $12 million in cash and stock.<ref name="cks">{{cite web |url=https://theorg.com/org/turntable-fm/org-chart/seth-goldstein |title=Seth Goldstein |publisher=The Org}}</ref>

### Flatiron Partners (1998–2001)

In 1998, Goldstein joined venture capital firm Flatiron Partners, founded by Fred Wilson and Jerry Colonna. As an entrepreneur-in-residence and investment principal, he built and managed a $75 million portfolio of pervasive computing investments.<ref name="flatiron">{{cite web |url=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seth_Goldstein |title=Seth Goldstein |publisher=Wikipedia}}</ref>

During this period, Goldstein was an early investor in del.icio.us (acquired by Yahoo! in 2005) and Etherpad (acquired by Google in 2009).

### Majestic Research (2003–2010)

Following the dot-com collapse, Goldstein launched Majestic Research in 2003. The company used data from sources including ComScore to generate proprietary investment research for buy-side funds.<ref name="majestic">{{cite news |url=https://www.nytimes.com |title=Investment Technology Group Acquires Majestic Research |publisher=The New York Times |date=2010}}</ref>

Goldstein served as founding CEO and later chairman until 2010, when Majestic Research was acquired by Investment Technology Group (ITG) for $75 million.

### Turntable.fm (2010–2013)

In 2010, Goldstein partnered with developer Billy Chasen to launch Stickybits, a "digital graffiti" application that allowed users to attach digital media to physical objects using barcodes. After struggling to find product-market fit, the team pivoted in 2011 to create Turntable.fm, a social music platform that allowed users to play music together in virtual rooms as DJ avatars.<ref name="turntable-techcrunch">{{cite news |url=https://techcrunch.com |title=Turntable.fm raises funding |publisher=TechCrunch |date=2011}}</ref>

The platform attracted over 600,000 users in four months and raised $7 million in funding at a $37 million valuation. Turntable.fm pioneered the concept of synchronous social music listening and influenced later products including Spotify's listening party features.

### Bright Moments (2021–present)

In 2021, Goldstein founded Bright Moments, a digital art gallery and on-chain DAO specializing in live, in-person NFT minting experiences. The organization pioneered "IRL minting," where collectors would witness generative art being created on blockchain in real-time at physical gallery locations.<ref name="bright-moments">{{cite web |url=https://log.fakewhale.xyz/bright-moments-founder-seth-goldstein-in-conversation-with-fakewhale/ |title=Bright Moments Founder Seth Goldstein in Conversation with Fakewhale |publisher=Fakewhale LOG}}</ref>

Under Goldstein's leadership, Bright Moments expanded to nine cities including Los Angeles, New York, Berlin, London, Tokyo, Mexico City, and São Paulo. The organization has minted collections by notable generative artists including Tyler Hobbs, Zancan, and Casey Reas.<ref name="edge-nft">{{cite podcast |url=https://www.edgeofnft.com/podcasts/seth-goldstein-of-bright-moments-the-physical-nft-gallery-dao-plus-berkeley-nobel-data-nft-nft-high-school-athletics-nft-in-travel-ikonick-bravado-deal-and-more |title=Seth Goldstein Of Bright Moments, The Physical NFT Gallery DAO |publisher=Edge of NFT}}</ref>

### Eden and Spirit Protocol (2023–present)

In 2023, Goldstein founded Eden, a platform for creating and deploying autonomous AI creative agents.<ref name="eden">{{cite web |url=https://eden.art |title=Eden - AI Creative Agents |publisher=Eden}}</ref> The company developed Spirit Protocol, a framework enabling AI agents to operate with persistent identity, memory, and economic autonomy on blockchain infrastructure.

Eden's work includes Solienne, an AI artist that publishes daily news manifestos, and has supported exhibitions at Art Basel Miami, Paris Photo, and ZKM Center for Art and Media Karlsruhe.<ref name="zkm">{{cite web |url=https://zkm.de |title=Solienne Exhibition |publisher=ZKM Center for Art and Media}}</ref>

## Industry leadership

Goldstein served as founding co-chairman of the Interactive Advertising Bureau's (IAB) User Generated Content and Social Media committee, which established standard definitions, metrics, and best practices for the social media advertising industry.<ref name="iab">{{cite web |url=https://iab.com |title=IAB Social Media Committee |publisher=Interactive Advertising Bureau}}</ref>

He is a frequent speaker at technology and art conferences and has been featured in publications including TechCrunch, The New York Times, CNN, and Wired.

## Personal life

Goldstein lives in San Francisco, California with his partner, artist Kristina Skovsted. He is known for his practice of "vibecodings," building software projects with AI coding assistants, having logged over 190 consecutive days of building as of 2025.
